Celebrating Muhammad Ali

This tribute page is dedicated to the memory of Muhammad Ali, the legendary boxer, and an inspiring figure who left an indelible mark on the world of sports, civil rights, and global peace.

Here's a timeline of Muhammad Ali's life

  • 1942 - Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. is born in Louisville, Kentucky, USA.
  • 1954 - Begins boxing at the age of 12 after his bike was stolen and he reported it to a local police officer, Joe Martin, who also trained young boxers.
  • 1960 - Wins a gold medal in the light heavyweight boxing division at the Summer Olympics in Rome, Italy.
  • 1964 - Converts to Islam and changes his name to Muhammad Ali. He defeats Sonny Liston to become the World Heavyweight Champion for the first time.
  • 1966 - Refuses to be drafted into the United States military during the Vietnam War, citing his religious beliefs and opposition to the war. As a result, he is stripped of his boxing titles and banned from boxing for several years.
  • 1970 - Returns to boxing and defeats Jerry Quarry in his comeback fight.
  • 1971 - Fights Joe Frazier in the "Fight of the Century" and suffers his first professional loss.
  • 1974 - Defeats George Foreman in the "Rumble in the Jungle" in Kinshasa, Zaire (now Democratic Republic of the Congo), reclaiming the World Heavyweight Championship.
  • 1975 - Wins the "Thrilla in Manila" against Joe Frazier in the Philippines, considered one of the greatest boxing matches in history.
  • 1981 - Retires from professional boxing with a record of 56 wins (37 by knockout) and 5 losses.
  • 1984 - Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease, which is believed to be related to his boxing career.
  • 1996 - Lights the Olympic cauldron at the Summer Olympics in Atlanta, USA.
  • 2005 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President George W. Bush.
  • 2016 - Dies at the age of 74 in Scottsdale, Arizona, USA.
